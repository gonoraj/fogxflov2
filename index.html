art inspires art

the

introduction

We all experience the world differently. The physical ways in which we see, hear, touch, smell, and taste. The mental ways in which we feel, relate, judge, and perceive. Alone in the thick fog, we are left with only ourselves and our senses to perceive what is happening. No two people experience the fog exactly the same way. This individuality is what makes us unique. Externally, the experience may look the same but we all process things differently internally. Based on these perceptions, we inevitably bring our own approach to remember the fog.

Our efforts to recreate and capture our experiences leads to the invention of new pieces altogether. It may not be intentional, but by expressing or capturing our particular point of view, we are collaborating with the world to diversify the piece. The goal of this project is to capture the different ways people document their time with fogxflo. In addition to my own experiences and recordings, I utilized various social networking sites to collect the experiences of a variety of visitors. Specifically, how the experience is then translated to the digital space. Documented below are the new pieces visitors have created after visiting the exhibit.

As you scroll through the locations, take the white space in between to clear your mind and prepare for the next site. Each site is unique in it‚Äôs own way. Just like being blinded by the fog of the exhibit, you will emerge in a new environment. The common uniter amongst all of the sites, the fog, will give you time to reflect on your own perceptions. You will be able to see what you weren‚Äôt able to before, and you will have a chance to experience some clarity of mind in the absence of others influence. Nobody will experience this exactly as you will.

the location

olmstead park

My

Experience

I arrived at fogxisland on a rainy day. For the time being, the rain had stopped and we were left with heavy air around us. I was worried that the exhibit wouldn‚Äôt be running. I had to search for this site much harder than any of the others. In this case, the nozzles were hidden away on an island that is not accessible to the public. To make matters worse, the rain and humidity of the day had done a great job creating natural fog as well. I looked around me for any indication I was in the right place, and only saw a mother and her daughter drawing on a bench. It wasn‚Äôt until the fog started that I was actually able to pinpoint the exhibit. I followed the fog, and watched it roll across the water in waves towards me. I could especially feel the dampness of the fog on my skin. On my raincoat, the big fat beads of condensation rolled down my sleeves. As the fog dissipated, it spread out over the area getting thinner and thinner until it was gone. Throughout the performance, the mother and daughter kept their heads down drawing, never acknowledging the fog that gently blanketed them.

fogxruins

the location

franklin park

My

Experience

I thought that fogxruins was the most exciting of the sites. With the most fog, and most area covered, it is a treasure trove of moments to be discovered. I could play around with different positions in and around the fog, since it was so constant. I could see other people weaving in and out of the structure when the fog was thinner, but then another row would switch on and my vision would once again be clouded. It was always uncertain how much fog there would be at once, and from what direction. I liked the spontaneity, and it felt like the fog had a mind of its own. Around the space were ruins of stairs. When the fog rolled close to the ground, the people on top of those stairs looked like they were resting in a cloud.

fogxcanopy

the location

back bay fens

My

Experience

Without a doubt fogxcanopy is where I saw the most people. Located in the fens, and right next to a bustling road and a well used athletic complex, there was always a steady trickle of visitors. Despite all of the people there, when the fog went on, you couldn‚Äôt see or hear them. Despite the fact that we were all existing in a shared space, once we were in the fog it felt intimate and private. Many of the people at this site were taking photos, trying to get the perfect shot for instagram or snapchat. On the half hour, just as quickly as those people had come, they disappeared. It was a bit of a spectacle, a photo-op. I think that those people that hurried off missed the best part, when the fog rolls down onto the small pond and gently rests on top of the water.
